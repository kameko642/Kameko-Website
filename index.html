    Canidate for a B.S Degree in Computer Science

    I had this grand life plan, you see – the classic "follow-in-the-footsteps" routine. I was all set to don the white coat, parade through college, snag a spot at NYU Medical School, waltz through a four-year anesthesia residency, and top it all off with a year in obstetric anesthesia fellowship. And just for kicks, I'd manage to be successful, brainy, witty, smoking hot, and married by the ripe old age of 28. Now picture the scene: first day of school, and bam! I casually dropped the bomb that I'd switched tracks from biology to computer science. Oh, the look on my folks' faces – you'd think I'd declared I was joining a circus instead. They couldn't fathom how someone who practically lived and breathed biology, was head honcho in science clubs, and did some research dabbling, ended up in the realm of ones and zeroes. Little did they know, those science club shenanigans and research escapades introduced me to Python – my first code crush. It was like a whirlwind romance – fast, bewildering, and utterly captivating. And trust me, that Python thing had me hooked. So, after that revelation, I spent a good two years as a research sidekick at Iona College, plunging into the abyss of computational chemistry. I sharpened my coding chops, juggled algorithms, and even learned some new tricks – talk about multitasking. Now, here I am, ready to kick-start this brand-new escapade. I'm all revved up to whip up some mind-blowing projects, lay the groundwork, and sculpt my path as a future software virtuoso.
